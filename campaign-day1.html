This is a civilisation location — Basha is rooted in something older than any trend.

Day 1 Day 2
Basha Week — 25 Days
I Production Brief
Location Al-Muizz Street, Islamic Cairo
Shoot Time 6:30 AM — First Light
Subject No main subject. Location only.
Format Stories only — 3 frames
Post Time 7:00 PM
Feed Post None — deliberate absence
II Camera & Shot Direction
Lens 85mm prime
Depth Shallow — walls soft, walkway sharp
Movement Completely still. Tripod or wall-braced.
Shot 1

The Hand on Stone

Close-up: a hand tracing along a stone wall. Texture of wall dominates the frame. Hand sharp, background blurred. The weight of centuries under fingertips.

Shot 2

The Silhouette & Door

A carved wooden door in background. A figure passes in front of it — partially silhouetted by morning light from behind. Presence without identity.

Shot 3

The Narrow Sky

Looking up — the narrow strip of sky between two ancient buildings. Pigeons in motion. The city waking. A sliver of light above centuries of stone.

Director’s Note

Shoot 10 variations, select 3 that make you pause. These are 2-second Stories frames — they do not tell a story. They make the viewer feel they have walked into somewhere meaningful. Stillness against movement of life is Basha’s visual language.

III Background & Atmosphere
Wardrobe — Background

3–4 real passers-by in traditional morning wear. No casting. No direction given.

  • Figure 1 An older man in white galabeyya carrying bread
  • Figure 2 A woman in black abaya moving with purpose
  • Extras One person walking through the stone archway at the far end, moving away from camera
  • Key Rule No one looks toward camera. Their figure shrinks as they walk deeper into the alley.
Environment

Medieval stone archways and carved facades. Narrow street funnels light into dramatic shafts. Just after first light, before the crowds arrive.

Visual Language

Camera completely still for all three shots. Stillness against movement of life is Basha’s signature. The only movement comes from people, never from the lens.
